# What your assistant does (and doesn't)

A quick, honest rundown so you know what you're getting on day one, and what's coming later. No
surprises.

## The one rule that shapes everything: you stay in the loop

The assistant **prepares, you approve**. It drafts emails, writes forwards, tees up calendar
entries, files your documents, and tracks your quotes and POs. You send, book, and decide. It never
sends an email, forwards anything, or changes your calendar or company systems on its own.

That's deliberate. It means nothing goes out with your name on it that you haven't seen.

## What it does now

- **Reads your inbox and tells you what matters.** A morning briefing: who needs a reply, which
  quotes and POs have gone quiet, who you haven't spoken to in a while.
- **Drafts replies in your voice**, ready for you to send.
- **Drafts forwards** for the small stuff, so passing a request on is one click.
- **Tracks quotes through to the PO**, and chases the PO, not just the quote.
- **Writes quote requests** to your estimating team.
- **Files your documents.** Drop a PDF into the `to-file` folder and it sorts it into the right
  project folder for that account.
- **Prepares calendar entries.** It hands you a file you double-click to drop the meeting into
  Outlook.
- **Turns meeting transcripts into actions** and drafts the follow-ups.
- **Builds your monthly reports** in your format, and asks you for any figures it can't find rather
  than guessing.
- **Learns your processes.** Tell it how a job really flows (`/map-workflow`) and it works that way.
- **Keeps notes on each account** that build up over time.

## Two things to understand about email

1. **It reads, it doesn't send.** It can read your Outlook and draft for you; you click send.
2. **The connection needs IT's OK, and it can't see attachments.** If your Outlook is your company's,
   their IT approves the connection once. The connection reads the text of an email but not its
   attachments, which is why filing works by you dropping the file in. Until IT approves anything,
   everything still works, you'd just paste emails in by hand.

## What it doesn't do yet

These are possible later. Some need more approval from your company. They're not in this version:

- Sending or forwarding emails by itself, or writing straight into your calendar.
- Grabbing attachments straight out of your inbox (that needs a small extra setup with your IT).
- Chasing things on a timer in the background, without you starting it.
- Sitting in your meetings live and taking notes (it works from a recording's transcript instead).
- Plugging directly into a CRM (for now, quotes and POs live in a simple tracker it keeps for you).

## The honest promise

This version takes the admin grind off your plate: the triage, the drafting, the chasing, the
filing, the reports. You stay in the driving seat. If it saves you a few hours a week and the drafts
genuinely sound like you, it's doing its job.
