# Email to send your IT team

If your Outlook is managed by your company, an administrator has to approve the Claude connector
once before you can connect it. Copy the text below into an email to whoever handles IT or Microsoft
365 where you work, and fill in the bits in [brackets].

The short version of what you're asking for: permission to connect the official Anthropic Claude
connector to your work Microsoft 365, so an assistant can help you read and draft email. It's
read-only. It can't send or change anything. It just needs a one-time sign-off from an admin.

---

**Subject:** Request to approve the Claude Microsoft 365 connector (read-only)

Hi [IT contact / team],

I'd like to start using an AI assistant to help me manage my inbox and calendar, mainly to draft
replies and pull together my account admin faster. It runs through Anthropic's Claude and connects
to Microsoft 365 using their official connector.

Before I can connect it, a Microsoft Entra Global Administrator has to approve it once for the
organisation. That's the reason I'm writing to you.

A few things that should help you assess it:

- The connector is **read-only**. It can read my email, calendar, and files, but it cannot send
  email, create meetings, or change anything.
- Access is per-user and tied to my own login. You can restrict it to just me, or a small group, in
  Entra.
- You can review the permissions and revoke access at any time.
- Anthropic's setup and security guides are here:
  - Setup: https://support.claude.com/en/articles/12542951-set-up-the-microsoft-365-connector
  - Security: https://support.claude.com/en/articles/12684923-microsoft-365-connector-security-guide

Could you let me know if this is something you're able to approve, or what the process would be?
Happy to jump on a quick call if that's easier.

Thanks,
[your name]
[job title]

---

**A note for you (don't include this in the email):** a security-conscious company will likely want
to review this properly, and they may say no or take a while. That's normal. The assistant still
does plenty without the email connection — you'd just paste emails in by hand instead of it reading
them automatically. So don't wait on IT to start using it.
